PlIF Imaging of the Tapered Elliptic 
A novel design aimed at reducing flow coherence 
and enhancing turbulence and small-scale mixing in 
the entire reactor is described%-this 
paper. The 
regular elliptic Jet, which was shown to have 
special mixing characteristics relative to a circu- 
lar jet, is mdified by adding a conical contrac- 
tion leading to the elongated slot outlet. The 
- combinatlon of this contraction with the noneircu- 
lar outlet have a substantial effect on the out- 
going jet flow. The elliptic jet is characterized 
by its axes switching in the downstream direction. 
The initial elliptic jet changes shape very rapidly 
due to the high spreading rate at the minor axis 
section. 
The initial minor axis becomes the jetls 
new major axis and vice versa at the other side. 
The flow of the tapered slot jet is very different. 
The spreading rate on the side of the minor axis is 
reduced. vhile the growth along the major axis Is 
increased considerably. At the same time, the flow 
accelerates at the major axis plane resulting in 
the formation of a double-jet flow. 
The centerline turbulence level of the tapered 
slot jet is UP to five times higher than in the 
circular Jet. 
In addition co the highly turbulent 
flaw in the vicinity of the fec center, the high 
turbulence activity in the jet's shear layer, i.e., 
at the jet periphery. is maintained. 
These flow characteristics are related to 
several different c~uses. The introduction of the 
oonsymmetric erimuthal disturbances into the ini- 
tial shear layer of the jet results in a very 
complicated flow field. 
The flow is unstable to 
numerous azimuthal modes leading to an eventual 
breakdown of the flow into small scale turbulence, 
thus enhancing the mixing at the fine scale. The 
double shear layer generated inside the flow due to 
the two jets which are formed in the major axis 
plane contribute to the additional Prod~ction of 
turbulence In the shear layer as well aa in the 
region. The third cawe is probably the dominant 
one and ie related to the inside structure of, the 
nozzle. The intersection between the conical con- 
traction and the slot introduces axial rotation into 
the Outgoing flow. The jet flow injected from the 
nozzle hen, in addition to the spanwise vorticity, 
an axlal component. The angular momentum of the 
axial vortices is determined by the transition sur- 
face betveen the conical contrsction and the slot 
outlet. 
It ia shown that by either increasing the 
conical contraction angle or by increasing the slot 
aspect r'stio, the intensity of the axial vortices is 
increased and the ensuing effect on the man flow 
field is higher. 
These t w  geometrical changes have 
similar effect on the aboos mentioned transition 
surface.
