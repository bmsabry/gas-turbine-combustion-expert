Functions of Top and Bond Coats

7YSZ top coat (TC)
•
Increase creep life of substrate 
by reducing steady state metal 
surface temperature
•
Reduce thermal stresses during 
transient stage (heat up and cool 
down)
•
Environmental protection
•
Increase bond coat creep life 
(reduced rumpling of bond coat 
at >DBTT)
•
Reduce cooling air requirement
•
Enable high TIT (efficiency and 
emission)

MCrAlY or aluminide bond 
coat (BC)
•
Oxidation and corrosion 
resistance
•
Bonding of top coat to substrate

Coating life determines 
inspection interval
~150°C

Failure Mechanisms for TBC Top Coat

TBC system prone to failure under 
thermal cycling 

TBC failure – immediate component 
failure from overheating for modern 
engine

Typical failure modes:
•
FOD or erosion
•
Top coating cracking (excessive 
temperature – phase 
transformation and sintering 
related cracking)
•
CMAS damage 
•
Hot corrosion of top coat (leaching 
out yttria and phase 
transformation)
•
Top coat spalling (TGO formation 
and cracking at the interface-
further discussed in BC failure)

Coating process, operating 
temperature, time and cycle 
dependent

Mostly experiment based life 
predication approach

Bond Coat

As bond coat or stand alone

Aluminide (β-NiAl, (Ni,Pt)Al, PtAl2+(Ni,Pt)Al)
•
High DBTT (600-900ºC)
•
CVD, pack cementation, slurry
•
Pt plating+ VPA
•
DVD NiAlPt alloy
•
Diffusion HT

MCrAlY overlay
•
β-Ni(Fe,Co)Al + γ
•
Lower DBTT (200-700ºC)
•
2-4 X higher cost
•
Coated component has service life 2-5 times of 
bare component
•
NiCrAlY-oxidation resistant
•
CoCrAlY-hot corrosion resistant

Compositions
•
Al: high temperature oxidation resistance
•
Cr: hot corrosion to sulphur containing salts
•
Y: oxide film adhesion
•
Pt: slow diffusion and improve oxidation resistance
•
Si: type II hot corrosion resistant

Bond Coat Related Failures

As standalone corrosion/oxidation 
resistant coating
•
Al forms Al2O3 (thermally grown 
oxide-TGO) during application, post 
coating heat treatment and during 
service
•
Al consumption due to TGO and 
inter-diffusion
•
When used alone TGO spalls and 
rebuilt until reaching critical Al 
content
•
Hot corrosion (molten salt)

As bond coat
•
TGO thickens over time (Top coat 
provide oxygen sources due to 
defects such as pores, cracks and 
oxygen vacancies)
•
Mixed oxide formation
•
Internal strain buildup

Challenges
 Material system - TBC less than ideal
•
TC: oxygen and radiation transparent, microstructure instability and 
limited thermal conductivity for EB-PVD coating
•
BC: higher CTE, brittle and high DBTT, use of precious metals (Pt, 
Rd…), composition variations

Process 
•
Lack of standard microstructure (process and vendor sensitive)
•
Complexity and variation of coating application techniques
 Life predication 
•
Semi-empirical in nature
•
Each case must be validated by engine experience and field 
feedback 
•
No universal model
