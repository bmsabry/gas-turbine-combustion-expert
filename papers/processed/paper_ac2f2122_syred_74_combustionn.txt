1. Introduction 
Swirl has been commonly used for the stabiliza- 
tion of high-intensity combustion [l-6] . 
The main effects of swirl are as follows: 
(a) To reduce combustion lengths by producing 
higher rates of entrainment of the ambient fluid 
and fast mixing close to the exit nozzle and on the 
boundaries of recirculation zones [l-6]. 
(b) To improve flame stability as a result of the 
formation of toroidal recirculation zones in 
strongly swirling zones [l-6 1.
