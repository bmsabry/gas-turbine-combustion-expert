Why Bearings Fail
¾ Most bearings are damaged in-service due to 
stress concentrations that arise from:
– Physical / dimensional discrepancies
….Misapplication, Mishandling, Defects
– Overrolling of debris
….Contaminants in lube oil
– Corrosion pitting
….Chemical interactions
Bearing Surface Distress
¾ Fatigue starts at surface defects
¾ Spall propagates at sub-surface cracks
¾ Rate of damage progression depends on:
– Bearing material properties
– Bearing geometry
– Load (contact stress)
– Rotational speed 
– Lubricant viscosity
– Chemical interactions at crack root (e.g additives) 
Oil Debris Sensor
¾ On-line, full-flow sensor fitted into lube oil lines
¾ Based on inductive sensing technology
¾ Detects 100% of particles above minimum particle size threshold
¾ Detects both  ferrous and non-ferrous metallic particles
¾ Counts and classifies (size and type) particles passing through the 
sensor
¾ Metallic debris particles from 
bearing sump flow past the field 
coils creating a disturbance signal 
in the sense coil
¾
3-coil, balanced field principle
¾
Signal characteristics define: 
- particle size (amplitude)
- particle type (phase)
Principle of Operation
Development History 
¾ Technology acquired from Canadian Government in 1991
¾ Selected for F22 fighter engine development program in 1992
¾ Introduced to aero-derivative engine market in mid to late 1990s
¾ Operational on more than 300 aero-derivative engines

Technical Challenges
¾ Measurement must be sensitive enough to detect the potential failure 
(P) so as to provide adequate time for the operator to take remedial 
action 
¾ Measurement must unambiguously identify the failure mode of interest
¾ Measurement must yield a P-F curve that is a recognizable and 
repeatable so that criterion for decision making can be established with 
confidence 
Qualification Process
1) Component Testing
¾ Failure progression testing of sample bearings under controlled 
conditions
¾ Assessment of measurement sensitivity, ambiguity and 
repeatability criterion
¾ Characterization of representative failure events
¾ Definition of in-service data processing and interpretation 
requirements
2) In-Service Validation
¾ Validation of measurement criterion under actual operating 
conditions
¾ Validation and refinement of data processing and interpretation 
methods

Commercial Challenges
1) The qualification process requires a sustained and purposeful 
effort over many years
¾ pay back periods can be long
2) The nature of the qualification process requires collaboration
¾ Technology provider
¾ End users
¾ OEM
¾ Government research organizations
3) OEM support is important - to facilitate and expedite the 
qualification process
4) The financial investment required is substantial 
Summary
1) On-line oil debris measurement has been successfully 
commercialized for aero-derivative gas turbine bearing damage 
detection and monitoring
2) Qualification process was a significant and time consuming 
component of the overall commercialization effort 
3) Key technical challenges were in the areas measurement 
sensitivity, ambiguity and repeatability – all of which needed to 
be substantiated by component testing and in-service data 
before decision making criterion could be validated
4) Key commercial challenges included collaboration agreements, 
OEM support and financial investment
